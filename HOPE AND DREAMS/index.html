"HOPE AND DREAMS"

SPOILERS FOR THE FINAL BOSS FIGHT FOR THE Pacifist Route

This is the track that plays when you fight the first phase of the final boss of the Pacifist Route in Undertale. It is the 87th track and has two BPM, 180 BPM in the game files, while in the original soundtrack it is 170.667 BPM. The keys it's in are B-flat major and B major with a time signature of 4/4. An interesting fact is that this track is reference twice in Deltarune, in the Legend of Delta Rune and in chapter 1 soundtrack "Field of Hopes and Dreams." While the track "Field of Hopes and Dreams" itself doesn't contain a leitmotif of HOPE AND DREAM it does have a similar style or format of it.

This track contains 3 leitmotifs. Which are: Once Upon a Time, Your Best Friend, and Dreams.

Once Upon a Time leitmotifs

6 of the 17 tracks that have this leitmotif are:

  • "Once Upon a Time" (All parts):
  • "Home" (0:36-1:44, part 1 and 2):
  • "Hopes and Dreams" (0:00-1:29 & 1:52-2:14, part 1 and 3):
  • "Final Power" (Hopes and Dreams reversed, part 3):
  • "SAVE the World" (At 0:00-0:14 & 0:35-1:46, part 1 and 3):
  • "Undertale" (At 0:37-4:41 & 5:20-5:58, part 1 & 2):
This leitmotif is considered to be the main theme of Undertale. It has three parts with part 1 usually occurring in all of the tracks that have this leitmotif. This leitmotif occurs mostly in the Pacifist Route connecting the main and important events that occur in this route, well, if you exclude the tracks "Hotel" and "Can You Really Call This A Hotel, I Didn't Receive A Mint On My Pillow Or Anything." This leitmotif is the most common occuring one, as it shows up in 17 tracks, thus making people consider this the main theme of Undertale.

This track is one of two leitmotifs associated with the character Flowey. This leitmotif also occurs in an unused version of the first section of "Final." This leitmotif shows the importance of this character during the tracks that they play in. It can also be considered to be one of Flowey's main themes, with the other leitmotif being Your Best Nightmare.

This leitmotif is actually a part of another leitmotif. The Snowdin theme has one leitmotif that are split into 2 parts. the first part is the Hope leitmotif, while the second part is the Dreams leitmotif. This leitmotif is considered to be a rather cheerful theme. Appearing in areas or situations that are well cheerful and not dangerous situations, simply a silly one, or I suppose a calm situation. So then why does it appear in the track of the final boss fight of the Pacifist Route in Undertale?
